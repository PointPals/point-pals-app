HOW IT WORKS

1. Set up your family — choose a companion character for each child from our cast of eight hand-illustrated mascots.

2. Together you pick chores and skills — make the bed, show kindness, feed the cat. You decide what matters and how many points each is worth. Narrow any chore to a specific child (great for "Leo feeds the cat, but anyone can empty the dishwasher").

3. Award points when you see effort — a quick tap gets it done. Sound and haptic feedback make the jar feel alive. Research shows that noticing effort (rather than praising outcomes) is what builds lasting habits.

4. Points fill a shared family marble jar — everyone works toward a reward the whole household chooses. No leaderboard, no competition.

5. A separate Kids' View lets your children safely see their own points, companions, and the growing jar — read-only, no login, no typing, no uploads. Designed to be handed to a child without worry.

6. When the jar is full, you claim your family reward and start a new season. The research-backed habit-fade system means the jar expands over time — celebrating the moment your child makes their bed without being asked.

WHAT MAKES POINTPALS DIFFERENT

— We're not a game. No hatching eggs, no dopamine loops. The goal is that you use PointPals less as habits take hold.

— We're research-backed. Every mechanic draws from Gordon Neufeld, Gabor Maté, Michaeleen Doucleff, and Kevin Leman. (See our About page for the reading list.)

— We're a family project. The jar is shared. The reward is something you choose together — screen time, a beach trip, family pizza night.

— Your children are not the product. No behavioural profiles, no ads, no data selling. Analytics from the Kids' View carry only an anonymous ID.
