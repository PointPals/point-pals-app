## 1. Strategy Overview

### Core message
> Chores that don't feel like a fight. A research-backed system that turns everyday responsibilities into rewards your whole family earns and celebrates together. Built for Kiwi families. Made in NZ.

### Three-pillar content strategy

| Pillar | Weight | What it does |
|--------|--------|--------------|
| **Connection** | 40% | Stories and tips about real family life — the hard bits and the wins. "Parent to parent." |
| **Research** | 30% | Bite-sized insights from the books that shaped PointPals (Doucleff, Neufeld, Leman). Builds credibility without being academic. |
| **Product** | 30% | Feature highlights, how-it-works demos, testimonials, trial starts. Always benefit-led, never feature-dump. |

### Pre-launch vs launch vs growth

- **Now (pre-launch):** Build awareness. SEO content (blog posts already live). Organic social. Collect email waitlist. Test ads with small budget.
- **Launch:** Paid social + Google Ads. Trial-offer push. First testimonials.
- **Growth (month 2+):** Referral mechanics, retargeting, nurture email sequence, seasonal campaigns (back to school, New Year).

## 2. Target Audience

### Primary: NZ parents, aged 30–45
- Household income $100k+ (can afford $12/mo subscription)
- Already tried sticker charts, chore apps, or reward systems — they worked for two weeks then fizzled
- Time-poor, guilt-prone, quietly tired of nagging
- Value evidence-based approaches but don't want something that feels clinical
- Active on Instagram (mums) and Facebook (mums + dads)
- Search "chore chart NZ", "behaviour chart for kids", "reward system for children"

### Secondary: Teachers, ECE centres, family influencers

## 5. Theme Song & Audio Production

### 5.1 Lyrics (written)

```
Sunny morning, marble bright,
every win is a little light.
Pip turns the page, Fern helps things grow,
Bramble's got a star to show.

Put your marble in the jar,
see how bright your family are.
One small thing you do today,
lights the whole family's way.

PointPals.
```

### 5.2 Production options (ranked)

| Option | Effort | Cost | Best for |
|--------|--------|------|----------|
| **ElevenLabs Music** | Low | Free credits exist | Quick demo / social Reels audio |
| **Local musician (NZ)** | Medium | $200–600 | Brand anthem. A real Kiwi musician can nail the NZ indie-folk sound. Post on NZ Musicians Facebook group or Fiverr filter NZ. |
| **AI + human polish** | Medium | $50–150 | Generate instrumental via ElevenLabs, hire a singer on Fiverr to record vocals over it. Best balance. |
| **Full studio (Ponsonby, Christchurch)** | High | $1500+ | If you go this route, you want recording, mixing, mastering, and a kids' choir for the chorus. Do this post-launch when you have budget. |

### 5.3 Musical brief (for any option)

| Element | Direction |
|---------|-----------|
| Genre | Acoustic pop / NZ indie-folk / children's album warmth |
| Tempo | 100–110 BPM (not too fast, not lullaby) |
| Key | C major or G major (bright, easy to sing) |
| Arrangement | Verse: fingerpicked acoustic (open tuning) → Chorus: add soft brushed snare, bass, glockenspiel → Outro: strip back to guitar + single voice |
| Instrumentation | Acoustic guitar, glockenspiel, light shakers, upright bass, soft brushed snare |
| Vocal | Warm female voice, slightly breathy, not Auto-Tuned. Optional: 3–4 kids humming the chorus |
| Duration | Full: 1:30. Edit to 0:30 for Reels. Edit to 0:15 for stories. |
| Ending | Single glass marble dropping into jar — *clink* — then silence |

### 5.4 Where to use the theme song

| Surface | Use |
|---------|-----|
| Instagram Reels | Background audio for all product Reels |
| Welcome page (pointpals.co.nz) | Auto-play muted, tap-to-unmute on hero section |
| App loading screen | Short 3–5 second loop (instrumental only) |
| Email broadcasts | Link to hear the song in welcome email |
| Apple / Google store preview video | Background track for 30s app preview |
