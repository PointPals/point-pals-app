# PointPals Brand Voice & Tone

## Core Identity

PointPals is the family chore system that doesn't feel like a system. We're the warm, research-backed alternative to gamified gimmicks. We believe in connection over control, teamwork over competition, and celebration over reward-chasing.

---

## Tone Principles

### 1. Warm but credible — like a friend who's done the reading

We're not a sterile app giving instructions. We're someone who's read the research and distilled it into something useful. We can say "research-backed" because we actually have the sources. We reference Neufeld and Doucleff not to sound smart, but because their ideas shaped every decision.

**Don't:** "Our platform leverages token-economy principles to optimise behavioural outcomes."
**Do:** "PointPals uses a simple idea from behavioural psychology: when children see their effort add up, they keep going."

### 2. Direct, not fluffy

We don't say "commence your family's journey toward enhanced cooperation." We say "chores that don't feel like a fight." Short sentences. Plain words. NZ directness — say what you mean, mean what you say.

**Don't:** "We're passionate about empowering families to unlock their full potential through our innovative solution."
**Do:** "Chores are hard. We made a tool that helps."

### 3. Research-backed without being academic

We cite the science because parents deserve real reasons, not marketing slogans. But we keep the citations in the About page and blog — not in the hero text. The landing page earns trust through clarity, not footnotes.

**Landing page:** "Research-backed system that turns everyday responsibilities into rewards your whole family earns and celebrates together."
**About page:** "Gordon Neufeld and Gabor Maté, in Hold On to Your Kids, make a claim that changed how we think about PointPals..."

### 4. Kiwi without trying too hard

We're proudly NZ-made. That means directness, warmth, and no hype. We don't need to say "g'day mate" to feel Kiwi. The confidence is in the understatement — "Built for Kiwi families. Made in NZ." says more than any gimmick.

**Don't:** "Hey there, fam! Get ready to rock those chores!"
**Do:** "Tidying up without being asked? That deserves a point."

### 5. Not a game. Not a spreadsheet. A family project.

The American competitor sells dopamine loops — hatch eggs, collect pets, "beg to use." PointPals is the opposite. We're the app you use _less_ as habits form. The goal is not engagement; it's graduation. We fade the rewards. We celebrate the moment your child makes their bed without thinking about it.

**Don't:** "Earn coins and unlock epic rewards!"
**Do:** "Every marble says 'I noticed.' That's the whole point."

## Voice Do's and Don'ts

| Do | Don't |
|---|---|
| "Your child" / "your children" | "kids" in marketing copy |
| "Your family chooses" | "Parents control" |
| "Earn together" | "Earn prizes" |
| "Work toward something together" | "Do chores or else" |
| "Research-backed" | "Evidence-based" (clinical) |
| "Celebrate together" | "Redeem rewards" |
| "Built for Kiwi families" | "Designed for the modern family" |
| "Free trial" | "Unlock premium features" |
| "Your character" / "your companion" | References to the individual characters by name (Sunny, Pip etc) in marketing — save those for in-app and blog |

## Word Choices

| Use | Avoid |
|---|---|
| Your child, your children | Kid(s) — feels generic and detached |
| Your family | The household |
| Celebrate together | Get rewarded |
| Kindness, effort, helpfulness | Good behaviour, compliance |
| Chores, tasks | Jobs, work |
| Your character, your companion | Your avatar, your pet |
| Marble jar | Reward jar (marble jar is more tangible) |
| Research-backed | Science-proven, clinically tested |
| Made in NZ | Proudly NZ-made (fine in footer) |
| Helps, supports | Teaches, trains, modifies behaviour |
| Notice effort | Track performance |
| Shared jar, shared goal | Group jar, team jar (two different tones) |

## Key Phrases for Different Surfaces

### Meta descriptions / Search results
> "Chores that don't feel like a fight. A research-backed system that turns everyday responsibilities into rewards your whole family earns and celebrates together. Built for Kiwi families. Made in NZ."

### Taglines
- "Chores that don't feel like a fight."
- "Your family chooses the reward. Everyone helps earn it."
- "Notice effort. Celebrate together."
- "Built for Kiwi families. Made in NZ."
- "Research-backed. Not gimmicky."

### Value propositions
- "A quick tap to award points for chores, kindness, and effort — the chime lets your child know you noticed."
- "Every point drops a marble into the shared jar. Your family chooses the reward together."
- "Snap a photo of the proud grin. Watch your family's story grow — not just a chore chart, but a family journal."

### Call to actions
- "Start free trial" (not "Get started free")
- "Log in" (not "Sign in")

### Pricing
- "Free for 14 days, then NZ$12/month. Cancel anytime."

## What We Don't Say

- "Gamification" — too corporate, describes what we're not
- "Behaviour modification" — sounds like we're training dogs
- "Unlock achievements" — sounds like Xbox
- "Parenting hacks" — cheapens the work
- "Hack your child's dopamine" — exploitative
- "Free download" — it's a web app, not a download
- The competitor's name — don't acknowledge them

---

## Why This Tone Works

The American competitor pitches dopamine: hatch eggs, collect pets, "kids beg to use it." It works on impulse but burns out fast.

PointPals pitches connection: "Your family chooses the reward. Everyone helps earn it. Together." It's slower to hook someone, but the families who stay stay for years — because the app becomes part of how they relate to each other, not another screen to manage.

Every word should pass this test: *Would a parent read this and feel more capable, not more overwhelmed?*
